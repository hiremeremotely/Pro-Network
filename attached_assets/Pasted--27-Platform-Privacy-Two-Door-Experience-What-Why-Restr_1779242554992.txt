#27 - Platform Privacy & Two-Door Experience
What & Why
Restructure HireMeRemotely from an open network (anyone messages anyone) into a managed placement platform where HMR sits in the middle of every company–candidate interaction. Candidates control what companies see; companies can only express interest through HMR, not contact candidates directly. This protects the proprietary candidate database and keeps HMR essential to every hire.

Done looks like
Company-facing profile view shows skills, experience, portfolio, and availability but never email, phone, or any direct contact detail
The "Message" button is replaced with an "Express Interest" button on candidate profiles when viewed by a company account
Expressing interest creates an admin-visible record in the backoffice dashboard — no notification goes directly to the candidate
Candidates see inbound interest only via HMR-mediated messages (admin can forward or hold them)
The messaging system is disabled for company → candidate direction; companies only see their own job pipeline and candidate shortlists
Candidates can still message each other freely (networking)
Admin backoffice gets an "Interest Requests" panel showing company, candidate, job title, date — with Approve / Hold / Decline actions
Approved interests trigger an HMR-authored introduction message to the candidate (from the admin account, not the company directly)
A privacy badge ("Protected by HMR") appears on the candidate profile to signal the protection model to both sides
Out of scope
Payment / billing for placement fees (tracked separately)
Candidate anonymization (showing initials only) — can be a follow-up toggle
Background checks or vetting workflow
Email notifications outside the platform
Steps
Profile privacy layer — Add an isCompanyViewer flag to the profile detail page. When true, hide email, phone, and any raw contact fields. Show only professional content (headline, skills, experience, portfolio, availability).

Replace Message → Express Interest on candidate profiles — When a company account views an individual profile, swap the "Message" button for "Express Interest". POST to a new /api/interest-requests endpoint that records { companyId, candidateId, jobId? } in a new interest_requests DB table. Show a confirmation: "Your interest has been sent to HMR. We'll be in touch."

interest_requests DB table — Add schema: id, companyProfileId, candidateProfileId, jobId (nullable), status (pending/approved/declined), note (admin note), createdAt. Push migration.

Backoffice Interest Requests panel — Add a new tab in /bo/dashboard listing all interest requests with company name, candidate name, job (if any), status, and date. Approve action sends a pre-written introduction message from the HMR admin account via the existing messaging system. Decline sends nothing.

Disable company → candidate direct messaging — In the messaging routes and widget, check if the initiating profile is a company account and the target is individual. Block the conversation creation and return a clear error: "Direct messaging is managed by HMR. Use Express Interest on a candidate profile."

Privacy badge on candidate profiles — Add a small "🔒 Contact managed by HMR" badge to the candidate profile header, visible to all viewers, reinforcing the trust model.
